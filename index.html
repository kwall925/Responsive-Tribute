Celebrating The Legacy of

Anthony Bourdain


A provocate Chef, boundless Traveler, and candid Educator.

Pivotol dates in the life
of Anthony Bourdain:

1956

Anthony Bourdain is born in New York to Pierre, a future Executive at Colombia Records, and Gladys, an editor at the New York Times.

1975

Bourdain drops out of Vassar College to cook full time, eventually attending the acclaimed Culinary Institute of America.

1998

After bouncing around kitchens and battling drug abuse and addiction for two decades, Bourdain lands his most prominent and well known job as a Chef, at Brasserie Les Halles in Manhattan. The restuarant would eventually close in 2017.

1999

Bourdain, at age 42, has his literary breakthrough, as his article "Don't Eat Before Reading This" is published in the New Yorker. The article spills the beans on some of the dirty and long held secrets of the culinary profession, and becomes the catalyst for his future fame and success.

2000

Bourdain’s Magnum Opus, Kitchen Confidential is published. The book is largely an extension of his infamous article. After intriguing, revolting, and infuriating many, the book becomes a best seller and leads to a television show.

2005

No Reservations is launched on Travel Channel. It is this show that brought Bourdain mainstream success and made him known to many outside of the Culinary cadre. Over the seven years of the show, Anthony exposed much of America and the rest of the world to far away locations and explored food and culture in the process.

2013

Parts Unknown debuts. The future Peabody Award winning show would feature Anthony's most important journalistic and educational work. Exposing the developed world to the realities of the daily lives of those often living in less priveleged situations.

2018

To the heartbreak of all that love and appreciated Tony and his work, Bourdain took his own life while on location in France.
